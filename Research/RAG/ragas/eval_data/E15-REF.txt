Spent the afternoon reading about tools for thought — Roam, Obsidian, the whole PKM rabbit hole. I've been down this rabbit hole before and it usually ends with me spending a lot of time setting up a system I use for two weeks. But this time it got me thinking about why I'm actually building REFLECT. It started as a school project. Now I genuinely care whether it works — not just technically but actually helps someone reflect on their life. That shift happened somewhere in the last few months and I'm not totally sure when. I think it's because I started actually using it myself, even informally. The tool changed when I stopped pretending I was building it for a hypothetical user and started building it for me. That feels like something important to hold onto.
