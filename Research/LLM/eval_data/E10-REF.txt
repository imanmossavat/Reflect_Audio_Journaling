I've been thinking about why I find it so hard to stop working at a reasonable time. It's not that I'm always in flow — half the time I'm just refreshing things, reading the same Slack messages twice, pretending to be productive. I think there's something about stopping that feels like admitting I didn't do enough, even on days where I clearly did enough. I wonder if this is tied to how I grew up watching my dad work evenings every night and treating it like a virtue. I don't want to replicate that pattern but I also don't know what the alternative model even looks like for me. What does "done for the day" actually feel like?
