# Research Papers

## Purpose
This folder contains research papers that provide theoretical and practical context for the REFLECT project.

These papers support decisions regarding:
- speech-based interaction
- AI-assisted reflection
- user experience in journaling systems
- digital diary usage in sensitive contexts

---

## Included Papers

### 1. ReflecToMeet – Structured Reflection Support in Meetings
A system similar to REFLECT that explores structured reflection in meeting environments using AI support.

**Relevance to this project:**
- Demonstrates how AI can guide reflection without taking control away from the user
- Provides insight into structured reflection workflows
- Serves as a reference for designing non-intrusive AI interactions
- Aligns closely with REFLECT’s goal of improving reflection quality through guidance

---

### 2. The Usability of a Digital Diary from the Perspectives of Intensive Care Patients
This study evaluates how patients experience and interact with a digital diary in a healthcare setting.

**Relevance to this project:**
- Highlights the importance of **low cognitive load** in journaling tools
- Shows how users engage with journaling in vulnerable or high-stress situations
- Emphasizes clarity, simplicity, and accessibility in UX design
- Supports the decision to keep REFLECT non-overwhelming and user-controlled

---

### 3. Experiences of Nurses with an Innovative Digital Diary Intervention in the Intensive Care Unit
This paper explores how healthcare professionals use and perceive a digital diary system in practice.

**Relevance to this project:**
- Provides insights into **real-world usage** of journaling systems
- Highlights the importance of **integration into existing workflows**
- Shows challenges in adoption and consistency of use
- Reinforces the need for REFLECT to be supportive rather than disruptive

---

## How These Papers Are Used

These papers are used as:
- **Conceptual foundation** for the application
- **Input for UX and AI design decisions**
- **Validation of the problem space and approach**

They guide:
- how reflection should be structured
- how AI should behave (non-intrusive, assistive)
- how users interact with journaling systems

---

## Key Takeaways for REFLECT

From these papers, the following principles are applied:

- Reflection should be **structured but flexible**
- AI must remain **non-intrusive and supportive**
- Journaling systems must have **low cognitive effort**
- Users must remain **in control at all times**
- Real-world adoption depends on **ease of integration and usability**
